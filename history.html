Our History

Highfield Primary School was founded on 8th February 1999, with a total of 12 pupils and 2 of them were Mr. and Mrs Mutanda’s children and 4 where their relatives. Their aim was making a contribution to the community and the general public by providing quality and affordable education. The School is a Private mixed day and boarding Primary and Nursery School, licenced and registered by Ministry of Education & Sports, with a UNEB sitting center. We are founded on Christian principle. The school follows the Uganda National Curriculum. Main subject taught include: (Mathematics, Social Studies, Science and English). Nursery Section, pupils are taught: (Mathematics concepts, General Knowledge, Reading, Writing, Drawing, singing, Independency, confidence etc. The school caters for the well- being of the pupils, health-wise, spiritually, socially and academically. In the school’s distinctive atmosphere, every pupil is celebrated as an individual. The School holistic approach to education develops skills and a love of learning that will last a lifetime. Our pupils benefit from an academic and innovative curriculum where they are encouraged to think independently and critically. They benefit from a vast range of Co-curricular activities, in clubs like: Pathfinder, debating, Young farmers, Music, Dance and Drama (MDD), Football, Netball, Volley ball. Thus, school engages the heart, head and hands of the pupils. The school endeavors to make its values (that is; team work, reliable, faithful, respectful, smart, love, transparent and God fearing), live in the day-to-day experience of every member of the community.

Our pupils are also:

  • Supported by a pastoral infrastructure designed to support them as individuals and help them mature into responsible citizens.
  • Encouraged to develop life-long learning skills.
  • Challenged academically based on their desire to learn.
  • Encouraged to discover and develop their talents.
  • Encouraged to think independently, creatively, and critically; to be inquisitive and to become active learners.
